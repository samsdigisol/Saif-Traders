What is industrial polymers?

Industrial polymers are large molecules made up of repeating units called monomers. They play a crucial role in a wide range of industries due to their versatility, durability, and various properties. These polymers are used to create an array of products, from everyday items to specialized components in different sectors.

What is engineering plastics

Engineering plastics are a group of materials that exhibit enhanced mechanical, thermal, and chemical properties compared to standard plastics. They are designed to withstand demanding conditions and are often used in various industries for their strength, durability, and performance. Some common examples of engineering plastics include polycarbonate, polyamide (nylon), polyethylene terephthalate (PET), and acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene (ABS). These plastics find applications in automotive, aerospace, electronics, medical devices, and more due to their ability to replace traditional materials like metal and glass in certain situations.
